2020 Calfire Season

The 2020 California wildfire season is a series of ongoing wildfires that are burning across the state of California. The season is a part of the 2020 Western United States wildfire season. As of November 24, 2020, over 9,279 fires have burned 4,359,517 acres (1,764,234 ha), more than 4% of the state's roughly 100 million acres of land, making 2020 the largest wildfire season recorded in California's modern history (according to the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection), though roughly equivalent to the pre-1800 levels which averaged around 4.4 million acres yearly and up to 12 million in peak years. The intensity of the fires has been increased by drying and heating from human-induced climate change, as well as decades of poor forest management.
